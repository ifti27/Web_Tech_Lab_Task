Automated Fan Depending on Temperature

This project focuses on the development of an intelligent fan control system that automatically adjusts its speed based on the surrounding temperature to ensure energy efficiency and thermal comfort. It utilizes a temperature sensor to continuously monitor the ambient temperature, while a microcontroller processes the sensor data and regulates the fan speed using Pulse Width Modulation (PWM). The system automatically increases fan speed as the temperature rises and decreases it as the environment cools down. Additionally, real-time temperature readings and fan status are displayed on an LCD screen, providing clear and immediate feedback. This project demonstrates essential concepts of sensor interfacing, control logic design, and automation through microprocessor programming. Overall, it lays the foundation for smart home and energy-efficient systems by showcasing temperature-based automation and the effective integration of sensors and microcontrollers for real-time environmental control.

Smart Parking Monitoring

This project presents a Smart Parking System built using digital logic circuits to monitor and manage parking spaces efficiently. Developed for the Digital Logic Design course, it operates without microcontrollers or Arduino, relying instead on combinational and sequential circuits. IR sensors detect vehicle entry and exit, while up/down counters track parked vehicles and comparators determine available spaces. A 7-segment display shows real-time parking status, automatically updating as cars enter or leave. Fully hardware-based, the system demonstrates practical use of logic gates, counters, and sensors, showcasing the potential of digital automation for efficient parking management.
